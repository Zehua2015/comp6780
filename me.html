Reflection: Learning Through Web Development
Throughout this semester's web development course, my understanding of effective website design has deepened significantly. Reflecting on my experiences and the feedback I've received, I've identified several key areas where my initial approach could have been improved.
User-Friendly Design and HCI:
Initially, I underestimated the importance of user-friendly design and Human-Center Interaction (HCI). User-friendly design ensures that website functions are clear and components are easily accessible. For example, while developing a website themed around the Star Wars universe, I used the well-known droid R2D2 as a clickable element. However, to enhance interactivity and engagement, integrating a storyboard where R2D2 guides users through the site would have made the website's purpose and content clearer. This adjustment would not only make the site more interactive but also improve its usability, making it easier for users to navigate and interact with the content.
Compatibility:
Another crucial realization was the importance of designing for compatibility from the start. A website should be accessible on various devices and browsers. Initially, I positioned elements using pixel measurements, which I later learned was restrictive to users with different screen sizes than my own. If stareted again, I should employ percentages and the "@media" rule for responsive design, which significantly improved the site's accessibility and ease of maintenance across different devices.
Engaging Content Based on User Feedback:
Finally, if I were to start over, I would begin with a user questionnaire to better understand what aspects of the Star Wars universe are most intriguing to my audience. This approach would allow me to tailor the content more effectively, ensuring it captures and retains user interest.
